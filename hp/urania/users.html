  • There is a nice application of Urania for sailors still using Celestial Navigation, mainly for those who use the method, which devised Capt. Thomas Sumner in 1837, and is called after him "Sumner lines of position". (BTW, this method was in 1875 improved much by Marcq St. Hilaire).

    Basically, this method starts from a guessed position, and then finds the true position by the intersection of two or more of the cited above lines of position. To draw these lines, one needs both the Altitude, and Azimuth of the observed objects.

    Now rather than making a lot of table lookups in Astronomical Almanacs and doing lots of boring calculations, one could use the handy HACLK tool of Urania, which in "real time" gives straightforward all needed values. Start the HP 49G, key in the values for guessed position, choose the object to observe with R.A., and declination, and fire HACLK! Don't forget about precise time, and all the nice correction terms which need to be applied to the observed Altitude :-)

    --- Ralf F., Germany, 03/2000
